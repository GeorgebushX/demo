George W. Bush

43rd President of the United States (2001-2009)

About George W. Bush

George Walker Bush served as the 43rd president of the United States from 2001 to 2009. A member of the Republican Party, he was previously the 46th governor of Texas from 1995 to 2000.

Born on July 6, 1946, he is the son of George H. W. Bush, the 41st president. His presidency was marked by significant events such as the September 11 attacks and the subsequent War on Terror.

Legacy & Achievements

9/11 Response

Led the U.S. response to the terrorist attacks on September 11, 2001, initiating the War on Terror.

Economic Policies

Implemented tax cuts and economic policies that shaped the early 2000s financial landscape.

Education Reform

Introduced the No Child Left Behind Act to improve education standards in America.
